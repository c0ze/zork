The Great Underground Empire — illustrated, narrated, and playable in your browser.

The original Zork trilogy, modernized into a graphic text adventure: every location is rendered as AI-generated art, scenes are narrated aloud, and atmospheric music follows you underground — all running entirely in the browser, no install, no backend.

How it was made

  • The original Zork source was released by Microsoft/Activision under the MIT License in 2025. The game runs from its compiled Z-machine story file via Parchment (the Bocfel interpreter compiled to WebAssembly).
  • Each room's text was extracted from the source; the location art is AI-generated from those descriptions, with four interchangeable styles (ink engraving, retro pixel, painted pulp, cinematic). Pick one with the in-game Style menu.
  • Narration is pre-rendered text-to-speech; background music is original, switching by region as you explore.
  • The whole thing is a static site — the interpreter, art, audio, and a small scene-controller are all that's shipped.
